Drunk Driving Intervention Research


Overview

All content is in development phases. This section will describe the purpose of the study. Below is the landing page of the website, which will contain content from the information-only condition (see condition labels in orange boxes below). The messages presented here, and described in the text, are intended to teach moderate, light, or nondrinkers the skills to intervene in drunk driving and to build the perceived self-efficacy so that they can do so successfully.

Landing Page Information (Information-Only Condition)

Texas led the nation in 2009 in drunken-driving fatalities, and ranks among the 10 states that have done the least to prevent these fatalities. More alcohol is sold per capita in Austin than in any city in Texas. As Austin grows, so does its alcohol sales. State data show that sales of beer, wine, and mixed drinks were $600 million more than last year, and since 2014 alcohol sales in Austin have increased almost 10 percent, while sales statewide grew only six percent.

Drunken driving among college students is a national epidemic
Nationally, 2.8 million college students have driven while intoxicated, or “buzzed.” As the Austin American-Statesman put it, the reckless use of alcohol on college campuses nationally is resulting in an estimated 1,700 deaths yearly, 97,000 incidents of sexual assault or date rape, and 599,000 injuries. Arrest for driving while intoxicated leads to legal and financial consequences, possibly not getting a job, and the embarrassment of telling your parents.

Read that again.

Is it any wonder the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention has declared alcohol abuse on college campuses a national epidemic?

Randomly Assigned Conditions

Each participant will read the information-only condition on the Landing Page, and then will be randomly assigned to either one of four conditions: behavioral modeling, verbal modeling, verbal persuasion, or information-only.

Behavioral Modeling

Participants will be shown a video of a model(s) intervening to prevent his or her friend(s) from driving drunk. This condition will depict a demonstration of the intervention skills and their successful outcomes needed to build perceived drunken-driving prevention self-efficacy and impart reassuring information that participants will succeed.

View Storyboard ►

Verbal Modeling

Participants will only listen to a model(s) describe, or model verbally, the intervention skills and their successful outcomes needed to build perceived drunken-driving prevention self-efficacy with reassuring information. As implied, the intervention skills will be described, not demonstrated.

View Storyboard ►

Verbal Persuasion

Participants will only listen to spokespersons, the same as the model(s) in the behavioral and verbal modeling conditions, reassure them that they are capable of intervening successfully to build perceived drunken-driving prevention self-efficacy without a demonstration or description of the intervention skills.

View Storyboard ►

Information-Only

Participants will listen to a spokesperson(s) read the information on the Landing Page above, such as the statistics and the legal, financial, and social consequences of drunken driving.
